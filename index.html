Topographic Factor Analysis of Undersea Relief

Propagation of the earthquake in the seabed

Topographical factor along the spread of the earthquake

The starting point for the realization of this project was the need to create meshes from bathymetric data with variable depth defined by the user, so that it was possible to study the influence of relief on the amplification of earthquakes, from simulations in FEM.

In addition to the depth value, parameters more adjustable by the user were defined for better meshing, such as: element size of the upper and lower extremity layers, sizes of the horizontal elements, number of vertical layers and application or not of properties material from the external file.

From these entries, files containing the mesh geometry and files containing complementary information for analysis, such as gradient, angulation and height of the upper surface, as shown in the images, are returned to the user.

After this stage of mesh generation I was directed to the development of the solver and the analysis / comparison of the results obtained.

The solver used the finite element method to solve the problem. For this, we use the Galerkin method, together with the Thomas algorithm and using a linear interpolation function to assemble the finite element method itself, without the need for commercial programs. However, to validate and test some results, programs such as Ansys and analytical development were used.

The project was carried out by a team of two Scientific Initiation students from the LAMCE / COPPE Laboratory with the assistance of the coordinator, with the aim of analyzing the influence of the relief of the seabed on the amplification of earthquakes.

At the end of the project, I presented the work at the first LAMCE / COPPE workshop.

Off Road Vehicle

In order to apply in practice the knowledge acquired in the classroom and improve my teamwork, in order to contribute to my preparation for the job market, I decided to join the Minerva Baja team.

Founded in 2002, Minerva Baja designs and manufactures vehicles for off-road environments (Off-Road) which participate in competitions organized by SAE, Society of Automotive Engineers.

I participated in the team for 1 year and during that period I witnessed all the stages of vehicle manufacturing. My participation, although it was not so technical, was of great importance for my learning, as it involved a lot of dialogue with all areas of the team and with external suppliers.

In the team I was responsible for preparing the presentations for competitions and sponsors, where I often had to talk to different areas of the team so that I could create a more complete and coherent presentation.

In addition, I participated in the manufacture of the fairing in the vehicle, conducting a material study, aiming to acquire the material with the best cost benefit, which presented the essential characteristics for the project, such as impermeability, high impact resistance and reasonable resistance to fatigue.
