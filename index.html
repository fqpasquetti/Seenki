Dynamics of Floating Structures

This work aimed at the initial study of the dynamics of floating structures on wave effects, serving as a basis for more complex studies after this one, as an example, the study on stability of offshore wind turbines carried out by LAMCE / COPPE.

Such simulation was done with ANSYS MECHANICAL APDL, where the desired parameters were introduced and the solver was modified to calculate the pressures exerted by the waves on the floating structure. These calculations were introduced based on Airy's Theory, a more elementary approach to the theory of surface waves of gravity, also known as linear theory or small amplitude theory. Developed by Airy in 1845.

Mesh Generator

The starting point for carrying out this project was the need to create meshes from bathymetric data with a variable depth defined by the user, so that it was possible to study the influence of relief on the amplification of earthquakes, from simulations in FEM .

In addition to the depth value, more user-adjustable parameters were defined for better mesh generation, such as: element size of the upper and lower extremity layers, horizontal element sizes, number of vertical layers and application or not of material properties from external file.

From these entries, files containing the mesh geometry and files containing complementary information for analysis, such as gradient, angulation and height of the upper surface, as shown in the images, are returned to the user.

This program was carried out in 2019 while I was interning at the LAMCE / COPPE laboratory at UFRJ, and was one of the stages of a project in partnership with Petrobras.
